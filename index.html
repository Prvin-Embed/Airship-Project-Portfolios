Professional Focus

Engineering for Elevated Awareness.

I work at the intersection of embedded systems, avionics integration, communication networks, and intelligent aerial platforms. My engineering focus is on translating complex mission requirements—such as multi-sensor ISR (Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance)—into robust, fault-tolerant hardware and firmware architectures.

Modern tethered aerostats operate in harsh environments, requiring precise attitude monitoring, persistent high-bandwidth telemetry, and absolute power reliability. Whether I am writing low-level firmware for sensor integration, designing control feedback loops to mitigate wind disturbances, or structuring ground-station communication protocols, my goal is to ensure platform survivability and mission success.

This portfolio provides a technical breakdown of LTA (Lighter-Than-Air) systems architecture. By analyzing the engineering constraints of large-scale platforms, I demonstrate my capability to contribute to the defense, aerospace, and advanced surveillance sectors at a systems level.

Why Strategic Platforms Matter

Unlike tactical UAVs limited by fuel or battery life, strategic tethered aerostats trade mobility for unparalleled persistence and payload capacity. Utilizing a stationary mooring station due to their massive scale, these systems deploy payloads that exceed 3.5 metric tons to altitudes where the line-of-sight horizon dramatically extends radar and communications ranges.

From a systems engineering perspective, the challenge shifts from propulsion and path-planning to thermal management, uninterrupted power delivery via tether, high-bandwidth fiber-optic data links, and continuous environmental monitoring to ensure platform stability during extreme weather events over a month-long mission.

Engineering Focus Areas

Dynamics

Flight Control & Stabilization

Unlike free-flight airships, tethered aerostats face unique station-keeping challenges. The tether acts as a massive physical constraint, meaning wind response behavior relies entirely on platform geometry, aerodynamic surfaces (fins), and active payload weight distribution rather than active propulsion.

From an embedded systems perspective, stability requires high-frequency IMU-driven attitude monitoring. We track pitch, roll, and yaw disturbance sensing to feed control logic. If an aerostat pitches severely, the avionics computer must log the variance, ensure sensor gimbals compensate to maintain target lock, and monitor tether tension metrics.

Integration

Payload & Mission Systems

Long-Range Radar

Integration of heavy phased-array or rotating radar systems. Requires managing massive power draw, liquid cooling interfaces, and high-speed fiber data links to ground processing units.

EO/IR Optics

Electro-Optical and Infrared multi-spectral turrets. Embedded focus involves gimbal stabilization logic, video encoding (H.265), and managing latency over the tether network.

COMMS Relay

Serving as a low-earth alternative to satellites. Engineering challenges include RF interference mitigation, antenna placement to avoid hull shadowing, and multi-band transceiver control.

SIGINT / ELINT

Signals Intelligence architectures requiring ultra-low noise power rails, massive data logging, and secure edge-computing nodes inside the payload bay before telemetry downlink.
